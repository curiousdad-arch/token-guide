First principles

A token isn't a word. It's a chunk.

Models don't read in letters or whole words — they read in chunks called tokens: sometimes a whole short word, sometimes just a fragment like "-ing" or "un-", sometimes a single punctuation mark. That's how the same system handles any language, typo, or made-up product name without breaking.

The part that matters

Two sides get counted — and one costs more.

Every exchange has two halves. What you send in, and what the AI writes back. Both are billed in tokens — but the reply is almost always the expensive half, and it's the one most people forget to think about.

● Input — what you send

Your question & any files

Everything you type or upload. A one-line question is cheap; a 50-page report attached to it is not.

Lower price per token
● Output — what comes back

The AI's full answer

Every word the AI writes is billed too — and a long, thorough reply can easily outweigh the short question that prompted it.

Higher price per token
4–5× On most models today, each output token costs several times more than an input token. That's why a chatty, over-explained answer is where cost quietly piles up — and why "just give me the bottom line" is the single easiest saving anyone can make.

Exact multiples vary by model — see the comparison table below — but output always costs more than input.

Where leaders actually have leverage

Two kinds of savings.

Some of this is everyday habit anyone can pick up today. The rest is infrastructure work — controls worth building once so savings apply automatically for everyone. The percentages are illustrative, but they stack, and they're ordered here by how much they typically move the number.

Anyone can do today habits that trim tokens

Start fresh for a new topic

Every message drags the whole prior chat along. Opening a new conversation for an unrelated task drops that dead weight.

~20% lower

Ask for shorter answers

Replies are usually the priciest part. A quick "keep it brief" or "just the bottom line" cuts the most expensive tokens.

~15% lower

Attach only what's needed

A whole 80-page report rides along on every turn once attached. Share the relevant section instead of the entire file.

~10% lower
Infrastructure controls worth building once, save automatically

Right-size the model automatically

Route routine tasks to a smaller, cheaper model and reserve the top model for work that truly needs it — without users having to choose.

~40% lower, on routine work

Trim history behind the scenes

Automatically summarize or drop old turns so long conversations don't keep resending everything from the beginning.

~25% lower

Reuse repeated context (caching)

The same setup and reference documents get sent on every call. Caching lets the system reuse them at a fraction of the price instead of paying for them again each time.

~20% lower

Batch the non-urgent work

Work that doesn't need an instant answer can run in bulk, asynchronously, at roughly half the real-time rate.

~10% lower
